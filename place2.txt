The Great Wall of China stretches over 13,000 miles. It is one of the greatest wonders of the world. Built over several dynasties, its purpose was to protect Chinese states. The Wall winds through mountains, deserts, and grasslands. Each section of the wall has a unique story. Some parts are well-preserved while others are in ruins. The construction began as early as the 7th century BC. Qin Shi Huang, the first Emperor of China, connected many walls together. Workers included soldiers, peasants, and prisoners. The Wall is made from stone, brick, and tamped earth. Some sections are wide enough to allow five horses to walk side by side. Watchtowers provided shelter and vantage points. The Wall symbolizes China's strength and resilience. Today, it attracts millions of tourists every year. Many tourists prefer visiting Badaling and Mutianyu sections. Hiking the Wall can be physically demanding. However, the views are immensely rewarding. It is visible from space, according to legend. A visit to the Great Wall is unforgettable.
